Biomedical CAD Research

Summer 2014

I worked on a team of 5 to collaborate with doctors at the Boston Medical Center to create 3D printed prostate phantoms to test radiation therapy for prostate cancer. We built 3D models of the prostate and surrounding organs using MR and CT images. We created a system to hold the 3D printed prostate to use as a phantom in radiation treatment. Using our models, the doctors are able to visualize radiation treatment plans on an accurate model of the prostate.

Our research team also worked with professors in the Boston University Anatomy Department to create 3D models of fetal skeletons. Using CT images of preserved fetuses, we were able to make a model of the skeleton and 3D print the bones. We cast these bones in a polyester resin to keep the small pieces in place. The 3D printed skeletons can be shared in classrooms across the world instead of using preserved fetuses.

What is Baja?

Baja is an off-roading vehicle club at Olin College that competes in Baja SAE events with 100 other schools from across the country. We build a single-person car from 4130 chromoly steel tubes and run it with a Briggs and Stratton 10hp engine.

My job on the team

I am the Body System Lead for our team. This position means that I'm in charge of the chassis, body panels, driver interface, and integration of the chassis with the other systems of the car such as the drivetrain and suspension. I also get to teach members of the team how to design in SolidWorks and run crash simulations on the car. Making sure the car meets all Baja SAE rules and insuring the manufactuability of the chassis also falls under my responsibility.

Projects at Olin

PianoBot

Fall 2014

PianoBot is a piano playing robot that integrates mechanical, software, and electrical components to make beautiful music. Built for the class Principles of Engineering, the robot takes in any MIDI file and uses Python to send notes to an Arduino which activates servos to play the keys.

I was the mechanical lead on this project. We lasercut the stands for the servos as well as the "fingers" attached to the servos and used SolidWorks to design all mechanical components. Our final prototype has 36 servos to play three octaves including the black keys.

To learn more, check out the project website at poe.olin.edu/poe2014/PianoBot.

Robotic Hand

Spring 2014

The robotic hand was the final project for the Mechanical Prototyping course at Olin. Our task was to build a robotic hand that could grasp a wide range of objects, but we were only given one DC motor for this task. My team created a hand using four-bar linkages actuated by a threaded rod on each finger. The four-bar linkages allowed for the fingers to close around objects of non-uniform shapes like plastic Easter eggs, 2" square ice cubes, and car keys.

The hand was completely designed in SolidWorks and manufactured using plywood, sheet metal, and 3D printed parts. We went through multiple iterations of our design before getting to our final design.

Sloth Toy

Fall 2014

For the course Design Nature, this was a team project of 5 people to build a toy of an animal for 4th graders. The toy had to transport something that the animal would realistically transport in nature. Our sloth carried leaves through a maze of trees to get to an injured sloth on the other side of the forest.

At the end of the project, 4th graders from a local elementary school came to Olin to play with our toys and rate us on the experience. The kids loved moving the sloth through the maze to help its injured friend - they especially loved the time limit we set to make the game more interesting.

Hopper Toy

Fall 2014

This was an individual project for the Design Nature course at Olin. The goal of the project was to design a hopping toy that was inspired by nature. I designed, machined, and assembled my hopper using laser-cut delrin. My toy, inspired by a grasshopper's legs, went a total of 2ft in the air and did around 6 backflips. One of the challenges of this assignment was to create a trigger mechanism that allowed for a delay between setup and launch.
